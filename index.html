Background

I'm an enterprise software architect who has spent 15+ years building systems where getting it wrong isn't an option: identity platforms at Ping Identity, industrial systems at GE Digital, high-availability infrastructure at xMatters.

In recent years I've turned my focus toward applied AI, specifically how large language models and retrieval-augmented generation can be deployed responsibly in regulated, document-intensive professional environments.

I'm particularly interested in the legal profession. Law is one of the few fields where precision of language matters as much as technical correctness, and where the consequences of an AI getting something wrong are real and serious. That constraint appeals to me as an engineer.

Currently based in Port Moody, BC, where I'm also building middleware for legally authoritative data systems.
